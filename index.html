The Life of Annie J. Easley

"If I can't work with you, I will work around you."

1933-2011

Early Life

Annie Easley was born to Bud McCrory and Willie Sims in Birmingham, Alabama. She was raised by her mother and had a brother six years her senior. From the fifth grade through high school, Annie attended Holy Family High School, and was valedictorian of her graduating class. At a young age Annie had interest in becoming a nurse, but around the age of 16 she decided to study pharmacy.

Personal Life

Easley had always loved dressing up. She wore stockings and heels almost everyday in college. Although there were no dress code in her work department, wearing pants as a woman during that time was still not normalized. However, she was one of the first to wear pants to work in the 1970s after talking to her supervisor about it.

Special Dates

  • Mathematician Annie J. Easley was born on April 23, 1933 in Birmingham, Alabama
  • Easley entered Xavier University in New Orleans, Louisiana in 1951
  • In 1954, Easley moved to Cleveland, Ohio where her husband's family was located
  • Leading member of the team which developed software for the Centaur Rocket Stage
  • One of the first African Americans to work as a computer scientist at NASA
